What Brick Builder Are You?

What's your PRIMARY motivation when starting a new build?

  • Seeing the finished model look exactly right, down to the last detail.
  • Getting it built FAST so I can start playing with it or moving on.
  • Creating a cool scene, character, or story backdrop.
  • Making something that does something (moves, lifts, spins, etc.).

How do you approach the actual building process?

  • Methodically: Sorting pieces, following instructions step-by-step, checking alignment.
  • Energetically: Dumping pieces out, building quickly, improvising if needed.
  • Creatively: Focusing on the overall look/feel for the story, maybe skipping complex details.
  • Experimentally: Focusing on mechanisms, connections, and how things function together.

What happens if you accidentally place a piece slightly crooked or off-color?

  • It MUST be fixed immediately. It throws off the whole build for me.
  • Eh, close enough! I probably won't even notice once I'm playing.
  • Depends - if it ruins the scene's vibe, I fix it. Otherwise, maybe not.
  • Only if it affects the function. If it still works, it stays.

What's more important in the final build?

  • Perfect accuracy and pristine appearance.
  • Playability and fun factor right now.
  • The story, characters, and overall atmosphere it creates.
  • That it actually works or does something cool mechanically.

Once the build is complete, what are you most likely to do first?

  • Admire it from all angles, maybe take pictures. It's done!
  • Grab minifigs and start an epic battle or make it zoom around!
  • Set up minifigs and start imagining/acting out a story or scenario.
  • Test its function, see how strong it is, or start modifying it to work better.
